🔒 IT Security Essentials
🔑 Password Policy
📏 Minimum 14 characters — mix upper/lower, numbers & symbols
🔄 Change password every 90 days (system will prompt you)
🔁 No reusing your last 12 passwords
🔐 Use the approved password manager — never write credentials on paper
👤 Never share your login with anyone — including IT staff
📲 Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
  • MFA is mandatory for all systems — no exceptions
  • Use the Microsoft Authenticator app (already installed on work phones)
  • Never approve an MFA prompt you did not initiate — report it immediately
  • If your MFA device is lost or stolen, call IT within 15 minutes
📧 Phishing & Email Safety
  • Check sender address carefully — not just the display name
  • Hover over links before clicking — verify the URL destination
  • No legitimate IT or vendor will ask for your password via email
  • Unexpected attachments from unknown senders = do not open
  • Use the "Report Phishing" button in Outlook to flag suspicious email
🖥️ Workstation & Physical Security
  • Lock your screen when stepping away — Win + L or Ctrl+Cmd+Q
  • Never leave sensitive documents on your desk unattended
  • Dispose of paper with member data in the locked shred bins
  • Challenge unfamiliar people in the back office — tailgating is a threat
  • Do not plug in personal USB drives or unknown devices
✅ / ❌ Quick Security Rules
✅ DO
  • Use VPN when working remotely
  • Log off shared terminals fully
  • Keep software up to date
  • Report anything suspicious
  • Use only approved cloud storage
❌ DON'T
  • Use public Wi-Fi without VPN
  • Forward work email to personal
  • Install unapproved software
  • Click links in unsolicited texts
  • Discuss member data in public

🛠️ Most Common User Issues & Self-Help Tips
🔐 Locked Account
Wait 10 min then retry, or call Ext. 5555. IT can unlock in <5 min during hours.
🖨️ Printer Not Working
Restart the printer, re-select from print dialog. If stuck queue, submit a ticket on helpdesk portal.
🌐 Internet / Network Slow
Restart your network adapter (right-click Wi-Fi icon). If branch-wide, call IT — may be a routing issue.
💻 Outlook / Email Down
Close and reopen Outlook. Check Microsoft 365 status page via IT intranet. Log ticket if persists.
🖥️ Slow Computer
Restart your PC first. Close unused programs. If daily issue, submit a hardware ticket for evaluation.
📱 MFA App Issue
Ensure phone time is auto-synced. Restart app. If code fails, call IT immediately — may indicate compromise.
🔗 VPN Not Connecting
Verify internet connection first. Disconnect and reconnect VPN. Ensure MFA is completed. Call if unable to connect.
💾 Can't Access a File / Share
Check that you're on the correct network/VPN. Submit an access request ticket — managers must approve.
📷 Teller / Scanner Device
Restart the device and USB connection. Use the device-specific guide in the IT intranet knowledge base.
🔔 Suspicious Pop-up / Alert
Do not click anything. Photograph the screen, then call IT Ext. 5555 immediately.
📋 When submitting a ticket, always include:
Your full name & branch Asset / PC tag number Exact error message or screenshot When it started Steps you already tried Urgency level
Good tickets get faster resolutions — be specific!
🚨 Critical Incidents — Report Immediately

These situations require an immediate phone call to IT (Ext. 5500) AND your Branch Manager.
Do not attempt to self-resolve. Do not delay.

🦠 Ransomware / Malware Suspected
Files with unusual extensions, ransom messages on screen, or sudden inability to open documents. Disconnect your network cable immediately — do not shut down. Call IT.
📧 Successful Phishing Click
You clicked a suspicious link or entered credentials on an unrecognised page. Time is critical — accounts must be locked within minutes to prevent fraud.
💳 Suspected Member Data Breach
Any unauthorised access to member accounts, PII, or card data — even suspected. Regulatory reporting (NCUA / PIPEDA) has strict timelines. Report immediately.
🔑 Credential Compromise / Unauthorised Login
Unexpected MFA prompts, login alerts from unknown locations, or notification that your credentials appeared in a data leak.
💻 Core Banking System Down
Inability to process transactions branch-wide. Do not attempt workarounds. Call IT and open an emergency incident ticket.
📟 Vishing / Social Engineering Call
A caller claiming to be IT, a vendor, or a regulator asking for credentials, system access, or wire transfers. Hang up and report to IT and your manager.
🖥️ Unusual System Behaviour
Camera light on without reason, mouse moving on its own, unknown programs running, or unexplained account changes. Potential remote access trojan (RAT).
📦 Lost or Stolen Device
Any work laptop, phone, tablet, USB, or card reader that is lost or stolen. Remote wipe must be initiated within the hour to protect member data.
📋 Critical Incident Response Steps
  1. 1 Stay calm — don't click anything else. Step away from the device if needed.
  2. 2 If malware: unplug the network cable. Do NOT power off the machine.
  3. 3 Call IT Ext. 5500 immediately. State: your name, branch, nature of the issue.
  4. 4 Notify your Branch Manager verbally — do not email sensitive details.
  5. 5 Document what you saw: time, error messages, screenshots (use your phone if needed).
  6. 6 Do not discuss the incident with members or non-management staff — await IT guidance.
