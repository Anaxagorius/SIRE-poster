§ 1 Purpose & Scope

This Information Technology (IT) Policy establishes the framework for the secure, responsible, and legally compliant use of all technology resources owned or managed by Valley Credit Union (Nova Scotia) (hereinafter "the Credit Union"). It applies to all employees, officers, directors, volunteers, contractors, third-party service providers, and any other individuals granted access to Credit Union systems, networks, or data.

Valley Credit Union is federally and provincially regulated and is subject to obligations under the Credit Union Act (NS), Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA), Anti-Spam Legislation (CASL), Financial Transactions and Reports Analysis Centre of Canada (FINTRAC) guidelines, and applicable Financial Consumer Agency of Canada (FCAC) directives.

  • Lead incident response and breach notification
  • Report cyber risks to Board quarterly

💻 IT Department

  • Implement technical controls
  • Manage systems, patches, and monitoring
  • Maintain network and endpoint security

⚖️ Compliance & Risk Officer

  • Ensure regulatory alignment (PIPEDA, FINTRAC)
  • Conduct annual IT risk assessments
  • Manage third-party compliance reviews

👤 All Users

  • Comply with all policies herein
  • Complete annual security training
  • Report incidents immediately

🏢 Branch Managers

  • Enforce policy within branch
  • Ensure access is revoked upon staff departure
  • Conduct periodic workstation reviews

§ 4 Access Control & Identity Management

🔑 Principle of Least Privilege

  • Users receive only the minimum access required for their role
  • Privilege escalation requires manager + IT approval
  • Administrative accounts separate from standard accounts

📋 Access Provisioning

  • Formal written request for all new accounts
  • Access granted within 24 hrs of HR confirmation
  • Role-based access control (RBAC) model enforced

🚪 Access Termination

  • All access revoked within 1 hour of termination
  • IT notified simultaneously with HR on departures
  • Shared credentials changed immediately

🔄 Access Reviews

  • Quarterly review of all privileged accounts
  • Annual review of all standard user accounts
  • Immediate review after any security incident
⚠️ Shared Account Prohibition Shared or generic user accounts (e.g., "teller1@valleycu.ns.ca") are strictly prohibited for individual login. All accounts must be uniquely attributable to a specific individual.

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

MFA is mandatory for: all remote access, core banking system access, email administration, cloud services, privileged accounts, and any access to member financial data. Approved MFA methods: hardware token (preferred), authenticator app (TOTP), or SMS OTP (last resort only).

⚠️ Password Manager Users are encouraged to use Credit Union-approved password managers (e.g., Bitwarden Enterprise, LastPass Teams). Writing passwords on paper or storing in plain-text files is prohibited.
§ 6 Network & Perimeter Security

🔥 Firewall & IDS/IPS

  • Next-generation firewall (NGFW) on all perimeters
  • Intrusion detection & prevention systems (IDS/IPS)
  • Default-deny inbound; allowlist outbound
  • Firewall rules reviewed quarterly

🌐 Network Segmentation

  • Core banking on isolated VLAN
  • Guest Wi-Fi isolated from corporate network
  • ATM network on separate segment
  • Teller stations on dedicated subnet

🔒 VPN & Remote Access

  • Approved VPN required for all remote access
  • Split tunnelling disabled
  • VPN sessions timeout after 30 minutes idle
  • Personal devices require Compliance approval

📡 Wireless Security

  • WPA3-Enterprise on corporate Wi-Fi
  • SSIDs do not reveal organizational name
  • Guest SSID on separate network with content filtering
  • Rouge AP scanning enabled

📊 DNS & Web Filtering

  • DNS filtering (e.g., Cisco Umbrella) enforced
  • Category-based web filtering for all users
  • Malicious domains blocked automatically
  • Override requires manager + IT ticket

🚫 Prohibited Network Actions

  • No personal hotspots/tethering on corporate devices
  • No network scanning tools without IT authorization
  • No installation of personal network hardware
  • No use of TOR or anonymizing proxies
§ 7 Endpoint & Device Security
Managed Devices
  • All Credit Union devices enrolled in Mobile Device Management (MDM) / Endpoint Detection & Response (EDR) platform
  • Full-disk encryption (BitLocker/FileVault) mandatory on all laptops and portable devices
  • Antivirus/anti-malware with real-time protection and daily signature updates
  • Operating system and application patches applied within 72 hours for critical; 14 days for high; 30 days for medium
  • USB and removable media ports disabled by default; exceptions require CISO approval
  • Screen lock activates after 5 minutes idle; password required to unlock
  • Automatic screen lock must not be disabled by users
Bring Your Own Device (BYOD)
⚠️ Limited BYOD — Conditional Personal devices may only access Credit Union email and calendar via approved containerized applications (e.g., Microsoft Intune MAM). Personal devices must not access core banking systems, member data, or internal file shares. Credit Union data on personal devices is subject to remote wipe upon termination or loss.
§ 8 Email & Messaging Security

📧 Email Security Controls

  • SPF, DKIM, and DMARC configured and enforced
  • Advanced threat protection (ATP) scanning all inbound mail
  • Sandboxing of attachments before delivery
  • External email tagged with [EXTERNAL] banner

🎣 Anti-Phishing

  • Phishing simulation campaigns quarterly
  • Report Phishing button in email client
  • Zero-click preview of links before opening
  • Executive impersonation protection enabled

📤 Data Loss Prevention

  • DLP rules block unencrypted member data via email
  • Outbound emails with SINs/account numbers flagged
  • Bulk mail to personal domains requires manager approval
  • Email auto-archive for regulatory compliance (7 years)

Credit Union email accounts must not be used for personal or commercial activities unrelated to employment. Forwarding Credit Union email to personal accounts is prohibited.

§ 9 Data Protection & Privacy

Valley Credit Union collects, uses, and discloses personal information in accordance with PIPEDA and the Nova Scotia Personal Information International Disclosure Protection Act (PIIDPA).

🔒 Encryption Standards

  • AES-256 for data at rest
  • TLS 1.2 minimum (TLS 1.3 preferred) for data in transit
  • No use of deprecated protocols (SSL, TLS 1.0/1.1, RC4, DES)
  • Certificate management via PKI; auto-renewal enforced

🗑️ Data Retention & Disposal

  • Member records retained per regulatory minimums (7 years)
  • Secure wiping (DoD 5220.22-M or NIST 800-88) before device disposal
  • Physical media shredded via certified destruction vendor
  • Retention schedule reviewed annually by Compliance

☁️ Cloud Services

  • Only IT-approved cloud services permitted (SaaS allowlist)
  • Member data must remain in Canada unless explicit consent given
  • Shadow IT cloud usage strictly prohibited
  • Cloud vendor SOC 2 Type II reports required annually
§ 10 Banking-Specific Security Controls
Core Banking System (CBS) Controls

🏦 Teller & Transaction Security

  • Dual-control required for transactions over $10,000
  • No cash transaction overrides without supervisor approval
  • CBS session auto-logs out after 3 minutes idle
  • All CBS transactions logged and tamper-evident

💳 Payment Card (PCI-DSS)

  • Full PCI-DSS compliance maintained at all times
  • Cardholder data never stored post-authorization
  • Card data transmission via P2PE-certified terminals only
  • Annual QSA assessment and quarterly vulnerability scans

🏧 ATM Security

  • ATMs on isolated network segment
  • Anti-skimming devices inspected weekly
  • ATM software patched via secure out-of-band channel
  • Physical ATM access logged and monitored by camera

💰 AML / FINTRAC Controls

  • Automated suspicious transaction detection enabled
  • Large Cash Transaction Reports filed within 15 days
  • Suspicious Transaction Reports filed promptly per FINTRAC
  • AML system logs retained for 5 years minimum

🔐 Online & Mobile Banking

  • MFA enforced for all online banking sessions
  • Session tokens expire after 10 minutes idle
  • Fraud detection rules reviewed and tuned monthly
  • API endpoints authenticated and rate-limited

📑 Segregation of Duties

  • No single user can initiate and approve a transaction
  • IT staff cannot approve their own change requests
  • Finance access strictly separated from IT admin
  • Annual SoD conflict review by Internal Audit
🚨 CRITICAL — Wire Transfer Security All outgoing wire transfers over $5,000 require verbal confirmation via a pre-registered callback number. Email-only wire transfer instructions will never be acted upon. Employees must verify unexpected wire requests directly with the requestor using a known, verified phone number — never reply to the requesting email.
§ 11 Backup & Business Continuity

💾 Backup Standards

  • Daily incremental backups; weekly full backups
  • Offsite/cloud backup replicated in separate geographic region
  • Backup encryption with keys held separately from data
  • Backup restoration tested monthly (sampling) and quarterly (full)

📋 Business Continuity Plan (BCP)

  • BCP reviewed and tested annually (tabletop exercise)
  • Recovery Time Objective (RTO): Core banking — 4 hours
  • Recovery Point Objective (RPO): 1 hour for member data
  • Alternate processing site identified and contractually reserved

☁️ Disaster Recovery

  • Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP) separate from BCP
  • Annual DRP failover test with documented results
  • Critical vendors included in DR communications plan
  • IT staff on-call rotation for DR activation

🚨 Breach Notification Obligations In the event of a privacy breach involving member personal information, the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada must be notified as soon as feasible under PIPEDA. Affected members must be notified if the breach creates a real risk of significant harm. OSFI/FCAC notification may be required for material cyber incidents.
§ 13 Third-Party & Vendor Management
  • All vendors with access to Credit Union systems or data must sign a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) before engagement
  • Vendor security assessments (questionnaire + evidence review) required prior to onboarding
  • Third-party access via dedicated, monitored accounts — never shared credentials
  • Remote vendor access sessions recorded; just-in-time access provisioning preferred
  • Critical vendors (core banking, payment processing) subject to annual on-site or virtual audit
  • Vendor access terminated immediately upon contract expiry or breach of agreement
  • Supply chain risk reviewed as part of annual IT risk assessment
§ 14 Physical Security

🚪 Server Room / Data Centre

  • Card key + PIN access; biometric preferred
  • Access log reviewed monthly by IT Manager
  • No unescorted visitor access
  • CCTV recording retained 90 days

🖥️ Workstation Security

  • Clean desk policy enforced — no member data left visible
  • Screens positioned to prevent shoulder-surfing
  • Privacy screens on teller terminals
  • Locks on drawers containing sensitive documents

📦 Asset Management

  • All IT assets tagged and recorded in CMDB
  • Annual physical asset inventory
  • Lost/stolen devices reported to IT within 1 hour of discovery
  • Remote wipe initiated within 2 hours of loss report
§ 15 Audit, Monitoring & Compliance
  • Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) system in place; logs monitored 24/7
  • All privileged account activity logged and reviewed weekly
  • Core banking transaction logs retained for 7 years
  • Network traffic logs retained for 12 months
  • User activity on Credit Union systems is subject to monitoring; users have no expectation of privacy on Credit Union-owned systems
  • Annual internal IT audit; biennial external penetration test by qualified third party
  • Vulnerability scans conducted monthly; critical findings remediated within 72 hours
  • Compliance with this policy reported to Board of Directors annually
⚠️ Monitoring Notice By using Credit Union IT systems, users consent to monitoring of their activity for security and compliance purposes. This monitoring may include email content, web browsing, application usage, and keystrokes on managed devices.
§ 16 Training & Awareness
  • Mandatory security awareness training for all staff upon hire and annually thereafter
  • Phishing simulation campaigns — minimum quarterly; results tracked by department
  • Role-specific training for IT staff, tellers, and management (PCI-DSS, AML, PIPEDA)
  • Training completion records retained for audit purposes
  • Staff who fail phishing simulations receive immediate remedial training
  • Security updates communicated via monthly IT bulletin to all staff

Legal & Fair Use Policy
Internet, Computer & Technology Acceptable Use — Policy DOC-IT-001-AUP | Version 1.0
This policy forms part of the Valley Credit Union IT Policy and must be read in conjunction with it.
✅ Purpose This Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) defines permitted and prohibited uses of Valley Credit Union's computers, internet, email, telephone, mobile devices, cloud services, and all other technology resources. It applies to all employees, contractors, volunteers, students, and any other users of Credit Union technology, on or off Credit Union premises.
AUP 1 General Principles

Credit Union technology resources are provided for legitimate business purposes. Users are expected to act with integrity, professionalism, and respect. Incidental personal use of computers and internet access is permitted within reasonable limits, provided it does not:

  • Interfere with work duties or productivity
  • Consume excessive bandwidth or storage
  • Violate any law, regulation, or Credit Union policy
  • Create any legal liability for the Credit Union
  • Expose Credit Union systems or data to risk

Users have no expectation of privacy on Credit Union systems. All activity may be logged, monitored, and reviewed at any time without prior notice.

AUP 2 Permitted Uses

✅ Permitted — Business Use

  • Work-related research and information gathering
  • Communication with members, vendors, and partners
  • Online banking and financial system access
  • Use of Credit Union-approved productivity tools (Microsoft 365, etc.)
  • Attending approved webinars and online training
  • Accessing regulatory and compliance resources
  • Professional development related to role
  • Videoconferencing (Teams, Zoom) for business meetings

✅ Permitted — Limited Personal Use

  • Brief personal internet browsing during breaks
  • Personal email access (web-based) during non-work hours
  • Online banking for personal accounts (brief, during breaks)
  • News and weather websites
  • Checking approved social media briefly during breaks (personal, non-CU accounts)
Personal use must never compromise performance, security, or professional standards.
AUP 3 Prohibited Uses — Comprehensive List

The following are strictly prohibited at all times on Credit Union systems, networks, and devices — regardless of whether the activity occurs during or outside working hours:

🔞 Pornography & Adult Content

❌ Absolutely Prohibited

  • Accessing, downloading, storing, distributing, or viewing any pornographic, sexually explicit, or adult content
  • Accessing any website, app, or service that hosts or promotes sexually explicit material
  • Any access to child sexual abuse material (CSAM) — this is a criminal offence and will result in immediate referral to law enforcement
  • Sharing, forwarding, or distributing adult or explicit images/videos — regardless of perceived consent
  • Online sex work activity, solicitation, or access to escort/dating sites of a sexual nature
🗑️ Garbage / Junk / Time-Wasting Sites

❌ Prohibited

  • Excessive use of entertainment streaming platforms (Netflix, YouTube, Twitch, TikTok, etc.) during work hours for non-business purposes
  • Online gaming, gambling websites, or casino platforms
  • Fantasy sports or sports betting platforms
  • Clickbait, tabloid, or time-wasting content aggregators
  • Accessing sites known to host malware, adware, or deceptive content
  • Subscription to junk newsletters or marketing content using Credit Union email
⚠️ Illegal & Harmful Activities

❌ Prohibited — May Constitute Criminal Offence

  • Accessing the dark web, TOR network, or any anonymization services
  • Downloading, sharing, or storing pirated software, movies, music, or any copyrighted material without authorization
  • Participating in or facilitating hacking, cracking, network scanning, or denial-of-service attacks
  • Accessing credit card or financial fraud forums, carding sites, or identity theft resources
  • Any activity related to drug procurement, arms trafficking, or human exploitation
  • Distribution of malware, ransomware, spyware, or any malicious code
  • Unauthorized access to third-party computers, networks, or systems
  • Cryptocurrency mining on Credit Union equipment or networks
🏦 Financial Crime & Fraud

❌ Prohibited — Regulatory & Criminal Breach

  • Unauthorized access to member financial data or accounts
  • Manipulation, falsification, or destruction of financial records or audit trails
  • Using Credit Union systems to facilitate money laundering, structuring, or layering
  • Sharing member PINs, account numbers, or authentication credentials with unauthorized persons
  • Insider trading or sharing non-public material information electronically
  • Bypassing transaction authorization controls or dual-control requirements
😡 Harassment, Hate & Discrimination

❌ Prohibited

  • Accessing, creating, or distributing content that promotes hate based on race, ethnicity, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or any protected ground under the Nova Scotia Human Rights Act
  • Cyberbullying, harassment, or intimidation of colleagues, members, or any individual via Credit Union systems
  • Creating or sharing threatening, abusive, or offensive content
  • Accessing extremist, radicalization, or terrorist propaganda websites
  • Sexual harassment via electronic communications (email, messaging, video)
🔐 Security-Undermining Activities

❌ Prohibited

  • Attempting to bypass, disable, or circumvent security controls (firewalls, filters, MFA, DLP)
  • Installing unauthorized software, apps, browser extensions, or plugins
  • Using personal USB drives, external hard drives, or removable media without IT approval
  • Connecting unauthorized network devices (personal routers, switches, hubs)
  • Conducting penetration testing or vulnerability scanning without explicit written CISO authorization
  • Sharing IT system passwords, VPN credentials, or MFA tokens with anyone
  • Leaving workstations unlocked and unattended
📣 Unauthorized Communications & Social Media

❌ Prohibited

  • Representing or speaking on behalf of Valley Credit Union on social media without authorization from Marketing/Management
  • Posting confidential, member, or internal Credit Union information publicly
  • Sending spam, chain emails, or mass unsolicited messages
  • Using Credit Union email to sign up for personal social media, subscription services, or commercial accounts
  • Disparaging Valley Credit Union, its members, staff, or partners in any public online forum
  • Engaging in phishing, spoofing, or social engineering attacks
💼 Misuse of Resources & Intellectual Property

❌ Prohibited

  • Running personal business operations on Credit Union systems or time
  • Using Credit Union equipment for freelance or secondary employment work
  • Circumventing software licensing agreements or using unlicensed software
  • Reproducing, copying, or distributing copyrighted documents, images, or software without license
  • Storing personal large media files (movies, music libraries, photo archives) on Credit Union servers or cloud
AUP 4 Social Media Policy

All Credit Union employees who use social media — personally or professionally — must adhere to the following:

  • Never disclose confidential, proprietary, or member information on any social platform
  • Clearly state that personal opinions are your own and do not represent Valley Credit Union
  • Do not post photos, videos, or descriptions of Credit Union premises, systems, or operations without approval
  • Official Credit Union social media accounts are managed exclusively by authorized Marketing staff
  • Do not engage in online disputes involving the Credit Union — report concerns to Management
AUP 5 Monitoring & Privacy Notice
⚠️ Monitoring Disclosure Valley Credit Union reserves the right to monitor, log, review, and disclose any and all activity conducted on Credit Union IT systems, including but not limited to: internet browsing history, email content, file activity, application usage, keystrokes, and screen capture. This monitoring occurs to ensure security, regulatory compliance, and appropriate use. Users have no expectation of privacy while using Credit Union technology resources. Evidence gathered through monitoring may be used in disciplinary, civil, or criminal proceedings.

Users who discover or suspect a violation of this policy must immediately report it to:

🖥️ IT Security Incidents

  • IT Help Desk: ext. 100 / helpdesk@valleycu.ns.ca
  • CISO (Urgent): ext. 101
  • Report phishing via the "Report Phishing" button in Outlook

⚖️ Policy / HR Violations

  • Human Resources: hr@valleycu.ns.ca
  • Anonymous Hotline: 1-800-XXX-XXXX
  • Reports investigated confidentially; no retaliation tolerated

🚨 Criminal Activity

  • Contact CISO and HR immediately
  • Preserve evidence — do not confront the individual
  • Law enforcement will be notified by management as appropriate
AUP 7 Consequences of Violation

Violations of this Acceptable Use Policy may result in:

  • Immediate suspension of IT access pending investigation
  • Verbal warning, written warning, or formal reprimand
  • Mandatory remedial training
  • Demotion, suspension without pay, or termination of employment
  • Civil litigation for damages caused to the Credit Union
  • Referral to law enforcement and criminal prosecution where applicable
  • Reporting to professional regulatory bodies where applicable
🚨 Zero Tolerance Items The following violations result in immediate termination and referral to law enforcement with no exception: accessing child sexual abuse material (CSAM); theft or fraudulent use of member financial data; intentional sabotage of Credit Union systems; knowingly facilitating a cyberattack or data breach.
